FAQ

What data do I have to upload?

We build visualizations from your Google Location History. When your Android device requests your GPS location, Google stores that information. Google allows users who have enabled location services on their mobile phone to download any existing data from the last 6 months. Upon sharing this data, it is stored anonymously. We respect your right to privacy, and do not associate an identity with the information you share.

I have an iPhone. When can I see my data?

iPhone users have fewer recorded locations then Google Android users. We’ll be adding support for additional location data sources come February 2015.

Can other people see my location history data?

All data is anonymously contributed to the project. While your data will be added into our global location history visualization, it will be in no way associated with your identity.

Does it matter what kind of phone I have?

Technically no, but you will likely have a much more in depth history if your data is from a location service enabled Android device.

What data is shared?

The .kml file you just downloaded contains a set of GPS points, in addition to the time at which they were recorded. This file contains no personally identifying information. Open the file in a text editor such as Notepad (windows), or textEdit (mac). You’ll notice that the only information is that file is approximate GPS locations in which you’ve visited in the past. We do not collect any information in regards to your current location, nor any search engine behavioural data.
